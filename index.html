3 Pillar Content System

You only need 3 types of content to build authority AND make sales.

Pillar 1: Authority Content(The "I'm the expert" pillar)
+

This is content that positions you as THE person in your space 🤓

What it does:

  • Builds trust
  • Shows your unique POV
  • Makes people think "damn, she gets it"

Examples:

  • "3 things I wish every [your niche] knew about [topic]"
  • "The biggest mistake I see [niche] making with [problem]"
  • "Here's what no one tells you about [industry myth]"
Where WMHQ comes in:Inside WMHQ, you learn how to create Authority Content that doesn't just educate, it positions you as the obvious choice when someone is ready to buy. You'll learn how to position yourself in a way that shares value without giving away your entire offer.
Pillar 2: Conversion Content(The "here's how I can help you" pillar)
+

This is where you bridge the gap between free value and paid solutions.

What it does:

  • Shows your process
  • Teases your framework
  • Makes people curious about your offer

Examples:

  • "Inside [your program], this is how we solve [specific problem]"
  • "The 3 step method I use with clients to [transformation]"
  • "Here's what you get when you work with me (and why it works)"
💡 Pro tip:You're not giving away the house here. You're showing them the what and the why, but the how lives inside your paid offer.
Where WMHQ comes in:Inside WMHQ, we teach you how to create conversion content that doesn't feel salesy, but consistently drives DMs, applications, and sales. We also break down the psychology behind buying, so you know exactly how to move someone from "interested" to "invested."
Pillar 3: Connection Content(The "I'm a real human" pillar)
+

This is the content that makes people like you, not just respect you.

What it does:

  • Builds relatability
  • Creates emotional connection
  • Makes people want to be in your world

Examples:

  • BTS of your day/business
  • Your journey/founder story moments
  • Hot takes and unpopular opinions
  • "Here's what I'm learning right now"
🪄 The magic:When you mix Authority + Conversion + Connection, you become someone people trust, want to learn from, AND want to buy from.
Where WMHQ comes in:Inside WMHQ, you'll also learn how to show up authentically without oversharing, using personal stories and vulnerability strategically while balancing relatability with aspiration.

What makes a hook work

The formula for a strong hook:

Pain/Problem + Promise Curiosity + Specificity Relatability + Intrigue

Bad hook examples:

"Let me tell you about my content strategy"(too vague, no pain point)
"Here are some tips for your business"(boring, no curiosity)
"Today I want to share something with you"(slow, no hook)

Good hook examples:

"This is for the woman who's tired of 'cute' jewellery and wants something that actually feels like her."
"Swimwear for women who are done dressing for other people's comfort."
"For people who are tired of living with pain like it's normal."

Platform-specific hook tips:

Instagram

  • Text overlays are KING (most people watch with sound off)
  • First frame needs to be visually interesting
  • You have 1-2 seconds before they swipe

TikTok

  • Audio matters more (but still add captions)
  • Fast paced openings perform better
  • You have 2-3 seconds before they scroll
💡
Your hook has 3 seconds to stop the scroll ⏰ If your text overlay or opening line doesn't create immediate curiosity or relatability, they're gone.
The Psychology Method

How to make people watch until the end.

Part 1 of 3 · Why retention matters+

Getting someone to stop scrolling is one thing. Getting them to stay and watch your entire video? That's where the magic happens. Retention rate = algorithm love ❤️ The longer people watch your videos, the more the algorithm shows them to new people 🥳 So this isn't just about creating "good content" - it's about understanding the psychology of what makes someone unable to look away.

Inside WMHQ, you learn how to understand WHY people watch (or leave), and how you can engineer content that keeps them glued to the screen. This works on Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts - any short-form platform 👆

Part 2 of 3 · The pattern interrupt+

After your hook, you need to interrupt their expectation. This is where most people lose viewers - they go straight into teaching without creating more curiosity.

🪝 The formula: Hook → Pattern Interrupt → Payoff

Examples of pattern interrupts:

The Contrast"I thought I needed better products. I actually needed better tools."
The Myth-Bust"Everyone says just 'think positive'. But I was thinking positive and still repeating the same life."
The Personal Reveal"I thought I wasn't cut out for business."
The Unexpected Stat"I didn't realise how much safer my pilates practice could feel with these socks"
Part 3 of 3 · The open loop+

This is the secret to getting people to watch until the end (and boosting your retention rate, which makes the algorithm show your content to MORE people). At the beginning of your video, tease something that won't be revealed until the end. This creates an "open loop" in the viewer's brain, and our brains HATE open loops. We need closure. So they keep watching to get the answer.

How to structure this in your video:
